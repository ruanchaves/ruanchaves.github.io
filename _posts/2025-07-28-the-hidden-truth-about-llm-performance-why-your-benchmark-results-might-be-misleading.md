During my <a href="https://www.um.edu.mt/library/oar/handle/123456789/120557" data-href="https://www.um.edu.mt/library/oar/handle/123456789/120557" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Master’s thesis</a>, I reviewed past benchmarks in the literature and came to a surprising conclusion: for reasons that I will explain at the end of this article, the gap between general-purpose models and models trained specifically for Portuguese was much smaller than previously thought.</p><p name="2dd7" id="2dd7" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">You are invited to interact with my findings on the <a href="https://huggingface.co/spaces/ruanchaves/napolab" data-href="https://huggingface.co/spaces/ruanchaves/napolab" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">Napolab Leaderboard</a>, where I’ve listed all my research results along with other benchmarks aggregated from the internet. The images in this article are taken from the leaderboard. If this research has been useful to you, consider giving Napolab a star on <a href="https://github.com/ruanchaves/napolab" data-href="https://github.com/ruanchaves/napolab" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">GitHub</a>.</p><p name="867b" id="867b" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">The leading models on the Napolab benchmark are Transformer models that have been carefully fine-tuned on the tasks. These are compared with LLMs evaluated in a zero-shot fashion (thanks to the <a href="https://huggingface.co/spaces/eduagarcia/open_pt_llm_leaderboard" data-href="https://huggingface.co/spaces/eduagarcia/open_pt_llm_leaderboard" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Open Portuguese LLM Leaderboard</a>). The LLMs would probably perform better against the fine-tuned models if they had been allowed to use few-shot learning or RAG on the training and validation sets for the test queries.</p><figure name="2c2a" id="2c2a" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--p"><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*Kt8slGeKnGOHLPvDOahHBg.png" data-width="597" data-height="584" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/800/1*Kt8slGeKnGOHLPvDOahHBg.png"></figure><h3 name="fd21" id="fd21" class="graf graf--h3 graf-after--figure">The Benchmarking Crisis: Why Results Don’t Always Tell the Full Story</h3><p name="3e75" id="3e75" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h3">There are two systematic issues with benchmarks, which were already present in the community before the advent of LLMs but became worse after their appearance: what I call the <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">investigation gap</em>, driven by the exponential growth in the number of models, and <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">data contamination</em>, fueled by a similar explosion in the volume of scraped training data.</p><p name="34b3" id="34b3" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">We will walk through these issues in the specific context of Portuguese NLP. As I will show, current benchmarks have significantly overstated recent advancements, and models trained specifically for Portuguese offer little to no real advantage over general-purpose alternatives.</p><ol class="postList"><li name="3bc5" id="3bc5" class="graf graf--li graf-after--p"><strong class="markup--strong markup--li-strong">The Investigation Gap</strong>: There is limited investigation of models that weren’t explicitly designed for specific domains. Many models trained on substantial domain-specific data don’t advertise these capabilities, leaving their true performance potential unknown even to their developers.</li></ol><p name="1335" id="1335" class="graf graf--p graf-after--li">One example of this investigation gap comes from <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.06721" data-href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.06721" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Advancing Neural Encoding of Portuguese with Transformer Albertina PT-*</a>, by Rodrigues et al..</p><p name="c414" id="c414" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">The paper, submitted on 11 May 2023, releases <a href="https://huggingface.co/collections/PORTULAN/albertina-66a39cf7e2460605f3f1a9c2" data-href="https://huggingface.co/collections/PORTULAN/albertina-66a39cf7e2460605f3f1a9c2" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Albertina</a>, a family of encoders for Portuguese, and claims a new state-of-the-art over the BERTimbau Large model ( <a href="https://huggingface.co/neuralmind/bert-large-portuguese-cased" data-href="https://huggingface.co/neuralmind/bert-large-portuguese-cased" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">neuralmind/bert-large-portuguese-case </a>) on the ASSIN 2 RTE and ASSIN 2 STS datasets ( <a href="https://huggingface.co/datasets/nilc-nlp/assin2" data-href="https://huggingface.co/datasets/nilc-nlp/assin2" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">nilc-nlp/assin2</a> ).</p><figure name="dbad" id="dbad" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--p"><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*C4EiKQtDinJ7wBv6bRZIaA.png" data-width="643" data-height="217" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/800/1*C4EiKQtDinJ7wBv6bRZIaA.png"></figure><p name="d648" id="d648" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">However, the Albertina paper failed to consider <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">xlm-roberta-large</strong> ( <a href="https://huggingface.co/FacebookAI/xlm-roberta-large" data-href="https://huggingface.co/FacebookAI/xlm-roberta-large" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">FacebookAI/xlm-roberta-large</a> ), available since 2019, and <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">deberta-v2-xlarge</strong> ( <a href="https://huggingface.co/microsoft/deberta-v2-xlarge" data-href="https://huggingface.co/microsoft/deberta-v2-xlarge" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">microsoft/deberta-v2-xlarge</a> ), available since 2021.</p><p name="0151" id="0151" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p"><strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">xlm-roberta-large </strong>is advertised as an multilingual model, and <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">deberta-v2-xlarge</strong>, although pretrained on massive amounts of Portuguese data, is advertised as an English model. Yet both models perform similarly to the Albertina models.</p><figure name="fb50" id="fb50" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--p"><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*rpk3Vwnv4m1UnDUrC61Irw.png" data-width="1173" data-height="385" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/800/1*rpk3Vwnv4m1UnDUrC61Irw.png"></figure><p name="c633" id="c633" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">On the radar chart, this becomes clear: the orange line, corresponding to <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">xlm-roberta-large</strong>, closely mimics the performance of the top model <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">albertina-pt-pt</strong>, and even outperforms<strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong"> albertina-pt-br </strong>in some cases.</p><p name="8f99" id="8f99" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Both <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">xlm-roberta-large</strong> and <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">deberta-v2-xlarge</strong> perform significantly better than the comparison model used in the Albertina paper,<strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong"> bertimbau-large</strong>.</p><figure name="3131" id="3131" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--p"><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*qyQO3salOJp0-An3Y3uqeA.png" data-width="936" data-height="700" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/800/1*qyQO3salOJp0-An3Y3uqeA.png"></figure><p name="40f6" id="40f6" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">2.<strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong"> The Data Contamination Problem</strong>: Perhaps the most critical issue affecting benchmark reliability is data contamination, the inadvertent exposure of test sets during model training. This can artificially inflate performance metrics, since the model has already learned from the test set itself, creating misleading comparisons between models.</p><p name="98d5" id="98d5" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">The risk of data contamination varies significantly across datasets. Newer, less widely-adopted datasets show lower contamination risk compared to well-established datasets that have been extensively replicated across the internet.</p><p name="b7ce" id="b7ce" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">A relevant example comes from the paper<a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.12049" data-href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.12049" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank"> Sabiá-3 Technical Report</a>, by <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">Abonizio et al.</em>. <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Sabiá‑3</strong> is a proprietary large language model developed by Maritaca AI for Brazilian Portuguese natural language tasks ( <a href="https://www.maritaca.ai/" data-href="https://www.maritaca.ai/" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="nofollow noopener" target="_blank">https://www.maritaca.ai/</a> ).</p><p name="3edc" id="3edc" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">The report compares <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Sabiá-3</strong>’s performance with two similarly priced competitors, <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Llama 3.1 70B</strong> and <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">GPT-4o Mini</strong>, on 93 Brazilian exams. <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Sabiá-3</strong> is a clear winner, scoring significantly higher than the others.</p><figure name="2df6" id="2df6" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--p"><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*pPAL6LWEqMrD9LZwYcXA9A.png" data-width="718" data-height="498" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/800/1*pPAL6LWEqMrD9LZwYcXA9A.png"></figure><p name="d3cc" id="d3cc" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">However, the Napolab leaderboard tells a more nuanced story. As shown in the radar chart below, although <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Sabiá-3</strong> outperforms its competitors on datasets widely adopted by the Brazilian NLP community (such as <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">ASSIN 2 STS</strong> and <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">ASSIN 2 RTE</strong>), it falls behind on lesser-known datasets like <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">FaQUaD-NLI </strong>and <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">HateBR</strong>, which are less likely to be contaminated.</p><figure name="f36e" id="f36e" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--p"><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*XW9IODnDCp8BCJDI9F9HcA.png" data-width="936" data-height="700" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/800/1*XW9IODnDCp8BCJDI9F9HcA.png"></figure><p name="a1f8" id="a1f8" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure"><strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">FaQUaD-NLI (</strong><a href="https://huggingface.co/datasets/ruanchaves/faquad-nli" data-href="https://huggingface.co/datasets/ruanchaves/faquad-nli" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">ruanchaves/faquad-nli</a><strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">) </strong>and <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">HateBR</strong> (<a href="https://huggingface.co/datasets/ruanchaves/hatebr" data-href="https://huggingface.co/datasets/ruanchaves/hatebr" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">ruanchaves/hatebr</a>) were reformatted by me in 2023 during my <a href="https://www.um.edu.mt/library/oar/handle/123456789/120557" data-href="https://www.um.edu.mt/library/oar/handle/123456789/120557" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Master’s research</a>. Although the original data was collected by others, I defined the splits, established the evaluation format, and published the <a href="https://huggingface.co/ruanchaves/datasets" data-href="https://huggingface.co/ruanchaves/datasets" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">datasets on the Hub</a>. While they’ve appeared in a few publications (such as <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.16640" data-href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.16640" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">TeenyTinyLlama: Open-Source Tiny Language Models Trained in Brazilian Portuguese</a>, by <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">Côrrea et al.</em>), they haven’t seen broad community adoption, making them less vulnerable to data contamination.</p><p name="9a2e" id="9a2e" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Although studies on data contamination exist in the NLP community, many domains still lack robust investigations. That’s because while there’s motivation to publish benchmarks that favor your own model, there’s less incentive to review the literature critically or challenge established results.</p><p name="d2e5" id="d2e5" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Researchers from my alma mater, the University of the Basque Country, have done excellent work in this area. Their paper, <a href="https://aclanthology.org/2023.findings-emnlp.722/" data-href="https://aclanthology.org/2023.findings-emnlp.722/" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><em class="markup--em markup--p-em">NLP Evaluation in Trouble: On the Need to Measure LLM Data Contamination for Each Benchmark</em></a>, by Sainz et al., is definitely worth a read.</p><p name="cb4f" id="cb4f" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">If you’d like to take a deeper dive into these issues, I recommend exploring the Napolab repository and my Master’s thesis: <a href="https://www.um.edu.mt/library/oar/handle/123456789/120557" data-href="https://www.um.edu.mt/library/oar/handle/123456789/120557" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><em class="markup--em markup--p-em">Lessons Learned from the Evaluation of Portuguese Language Models</em></a>.</p><p name="48b5" id="48b5" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p graf--trailing">The investigation gap and data contamination issues I uncovered in Portuguese NLP extend far beyond this domain. If you’re building benchmarks, be sure to challenge your assumptions. Progress in NLP depends not just on better models, but also on better ways of measuring them.</p>
