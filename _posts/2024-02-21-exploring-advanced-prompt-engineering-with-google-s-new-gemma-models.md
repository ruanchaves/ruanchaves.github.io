These models are based on the same tech and research used for creating the Gemini models. Gemma comes in two versions: Gemma 2B, which is small enough for the free tier of Google Colab, and Gemma 7B.</p><p name="8be0" id="8be0" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">This launch is significant because, unlike what other companies have released up until now, it claims to closely parallel a leading Large Language Model that is not open to the public.</p><p name="e559" id="e559" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Therefore, we may be able to employ advanced prompt engineering techniques on Gemma, which are not possible without an open AI model, and subsequently apply them on Gemini, a closed AI model.</p><p name="7eae" id="7eae" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">For instance, we could select candidate prompts with low perplexities on Gemma, and then use these prompts on Gemini, where we are not allowed to calculate the perplexity of each prompt. If both models are indeed similar, this approach could actually improve our prompts for Gemini, even though we don’t have access to the model weights.</p><p name="4c86" id="4c86" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">I am looking forward to seeing studies that experiment with this approach. If Gemma is as similar to Gemini as they say, we might see some great results.</p><p name="cbc2" id="cbc2" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p"><strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Gemma: Introducing new state-of-the-art open models</strong><br><a href="https://blog.google/technology/developers/gemma-open-models/" data-href="https://blog.google/technology/developers/gemma-open-models/" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">https://blog.google/technology/developers/gemma-open-models/</a></p><p name="464a" id="464a" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p"><strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Demystifying Prompts in Language Models via Perplexity Estimation</strong> <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.04037" data-href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.04037" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.04037</a> “Over a wide range of tasks, we show that the lower the perplexity of the prompt is, the better the prompt is able to perform the task.”</p><p name="106a" id="106a" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p"><strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Gemma on Hugging Face</strong></p><p name="bbc4" id="bbc4" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p graf--trailing"><a href="https://huggingface.co/collections/google/gemma-release-65d5efbccdbb8c4202ec078b" data-href="https://huggingface.co/collections/google/gemma-release-65d5efbccdbb8c4202ec078b" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">https://huggingface.co/collections/google/gemma-release-65d5efbccdbb8c4202ec078b</a></p>
