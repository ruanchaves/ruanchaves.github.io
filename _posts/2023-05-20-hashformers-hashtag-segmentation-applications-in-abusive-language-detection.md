<figure name="52a3" id="52a3" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--h3"><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*sJelFs5o7wIOsHvxuxOroA.png" data-width="450" data-height="327" data-is-featured="true" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/800/1*sJelFs5o7wIOsHvxuxOroA.png"></figure><p name="0baf" id="0baf" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">Abusive language detection, a critical aspect of modern NLP research, is often challenged by the lack of generalization across different datasets. In light of this, <a href="https://www.unibw.de/datensicherheit/personen/seemann" data-href="https://www.unibw.de/datensicherheit/personen/seemann" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener nofollow noopener" target="_blank"><strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Nina Seemann</strong></a> and other researchers from the <a href="https://www.unibw.de/code-en" data-href="https://www.unibw.de/code-en" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener nofollow noopener" target="_blank"><strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">CODE Research Institute for Cyber Defence</strong></a> at the <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Bundeswehr University Munich</strong> released recent papers called “<a href="https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s13222-023-00438-1" data-href="https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s13222-023-00438-1" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener nofollow noopener" target="_blank">Generalizability of Abusive Language Detection Models on Homogeneous German Datasets</a>” and “<a href="https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/natural-language-engineering/article/problem-of-varying-annotations-to-identify-abusive-language-in-social-media-content/B47FCCCEBF6EDF9C628DCC69EC5E0826" data-href="https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/natural-language-engineering/article/problem-of-varying-annotations-to-identify-abusive-language-in-social-media-content/B47FCCCEBF6EDF9C628DCC69EC5E0826" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener nofollow noopener" target="_blank">The problem of varying annotations to identify abusive language in social media content</a>”.</p><p name="62ff" id="62ff" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">This research demonstrates a fresh approach towards enhancing model generalization across homogeneous datasets. Part of their novel methodology was the deployment of the state-of-the-art <a href="https://github.com/ruanchaves/hashformers" data-href="https://github.com/ruanchaves/hashformers" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener nofollow noopener" target="_blank">hashformers library</a> for effective hashtag segmentation.</p><p name="cd27" id="cd27" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">The <a href="https://github.com/ruanchaves/hashformers" data-href="https://github.com/ruanchaves/hashformers" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener nofollow noopener" target="_blank">hashformers library</a> is open source and available on GitHub. The library has a <a href="https://colab.research.google.com/github/ruanchaves/hashformers/blob/master/hashformers.ipynb" data-href="https://colab.research.google.com/github/ruanchaves/hashformers/blob/master/hashformers.ipynb" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener nofollow noopener" target="_blank">Google Colab tutorial</a> and a <a href="https://ruanchaves-hashtag-segmentation.hf.space/" data-href="https://ruanchaves-hashtag-segmentation.hf.space/" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener nofollow noopener" target="_blank">Hugging Face Space</a> where it is possible to run and play with some of its hashtag segmentation models.</p><h3 name="d8eb" id="d8eb" class="graf graf--h3 graf-after--p">Why Hashformers?</h3><p name="a9e7" id="a9e7" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h3">The <a href="https://github.com/ruanchaves/hashformers" data-href="https://github.com/ruanchaves/hashformers" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener nofollow noopener" target="_blank">hashformers library</a> can effectively segment hashtags in any language with a GPT-2 model on the <a href="https://huggingface.co/models" data-href="https://huggingface.co/models" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener nofollow noopener" target="_blank">Hugging Face Model Hub</a>. This feature empowered the research team to manipulate German datasets in their research on abusive language detection. It’s important to remember that improved preprocessing often leads to better machine learning results.</p><p name="5be3" id="5be3" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Ultimately, the researchers were able to conclude that generalizability depends solely on the combinations of training sets and remains consistent regardless of the underlying method used.</p><h3 name="e120" id="e120" class="graf graf--h3 graf-after--p">Other Use Cases</h3><p name="edf1" id="edf1" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h3">This is not the first time that the <a href="https://github.com/ruanchaves/hashformers" data-href="https://github.com/ruanchaves/hashformers" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener nofollow noopener" target="_blank">hashformers library</a> is used in scientific research. It has already been used in a paper published at the <a href="https://lrec2022.lrec-conf.org/en/" data-href="https://lrec2022.lrec-conf.org/en/" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener nofollow noopener" target="_blank">LREC 2022</a> conference by Prashant Kodali and other researchers from Indian Institutes of Technology: “<a href="https://aclanthology.org/2022.lrec-1.782/" data-href="https://aclanthology.org/2022.lrec-1.782/" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener nofollow noopener" target="_blank">HashSet — A Dataset For Hashtag Segmentation</a>”.</p><p name="c612" id="c612" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">In this paper, they describe a novel dataset that features many hashtags in Indian English. They evaluated the hashtag segmentation capabilities of several models and found out that the <a href="https://github.com/ruanchaves/hashformers" data-href="https://github.com/ruanchaves/hashformers" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener nofollow noopener" target="_blank">hashformers library</a> was up to 25% more accurate than the other open-source alternatives available for the hashtag segmentation task.</p><h3 name="9e29" id="9e29" class="graf graf--h3 graf-after--p">Conclusion</h3><p name="2e79" id="2e79" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h3 graf--trailing">By integrating the <a href="https://github.com/ruanchaves/hashformers" data-href="https://github.com/ruanchaves/hashformers" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener nofollow noopener" target="_blank">Hashformers library</a> into their methodology, the researchers at the <a href="https://www.unibw.de/code-en" data-href="https://www.unibw.de/code-en" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener nofollow noopener" target="_blank">Research Institute CODE</a> effectively dealt with the problem of generalizability across German language datasets. This use case serves as a testament to the library’s versatility and power in dealing with language-dependent challenges in machine learning, showcasing its potential for broader applications.</p>
